"So, a common complaint about new construction is that it's all "Luxury" construction. What is "Luxury"? The marketing advertises granite countertops, hardwood flooring, maybe an in-unit laundry, or a doorman; but the thing is that everything has that now, except maybe the doorman. The difference between "luxury" and "affordable" housing is usually just the rent. "Luxury" is at best a marketing term for "market rate". The rent is the thing. It's all about the rent. The rent is the difference between "luxury" and "affordable". It's just the rent. ... It's the rent. So a developer can build a "luxury" building, or an "affordable" building, right? But they're roughly the same building. The code requires rooms of a certain size, with certain safety features, the construction has to be done in a certain way depending of the size of the building, so on and so forth. Some of the Real high-end luxury buildings, you know that's when you might start seeing much bigger apartments or bigger rooms or stuff, but your Regular "luxury" building isn't that different from an "affordable" building. So the main difference there is Return On Investment. And given the difference between a "luxury" building with a high ROI, or a nearly identical building with a low ROI, guess which one the developer is gonna pick. And even if the developer wants to build affordable housing, he's gotta get that past the banks who are financing the thing, and who also want the highest ROI, because it's less risk for them to finance."
